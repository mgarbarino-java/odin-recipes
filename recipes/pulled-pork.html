Pulled Pork

When I travel to the Carolinas I make to a habit to stop and try some pulled pork. I wanted to try and make it so I could have it any time that I wanted to. This is a recipe that I came up with with a little trial and error. Having some friends that are from the area helps with getting everything right. This recipe is for a crock pot so I can make this anytime of the year.

Ingredients:

  • 4 to 5 pound boneless pork butt shoulder
  • 1 large oinion
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 2 tablespoons of garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons of oinion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 2 or 3 bay leaves
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1/4 cup of apple cider vinegar

Steps:

  1. Wash and dry the pork but shoulder and trim the fat off.
  2. Slice the oinion and set aside
  3. In a large bowl mix together brown sugar, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, oinion powder, and cayenne pepper
  4. Run the mixture over the the pork butt shoulder making sure that you cover the whole shoulder
  5. Once you have the pork butt shoulder covered add the water and apple cider vinegar to the bottom of the crock pot
  6. Place the pork butt shoulder in the crock pot and pick the oioins over the pork.
  7. Add the bay leaves to the crock pot.
  8. Turn on the crock pot to low and cook for 8 to 10 hours or intil the pork reaches a temperature of 165 degrees
  9. Once done pull out the pork and fork pull the pork.
  10. Serve on buns with or without your favorite BBQ sause.
